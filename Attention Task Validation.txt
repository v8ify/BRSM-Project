2. Brief on Experiment & Methodology
 * Objective: To validate a newly developed "Selective Attention Game" by comparing it against a standard "Lab Task" (gold standard).
 * Design: A 2 × 2 Mixed Factorial Design.
   * Factor 1 (Between-Subjects): Target Load (Single Target vs. Multiple Target). Participants were randomly assigned to one of these two groups.
   * Factor 2 (Within-Subjects): Modality (Game vs. Lab). Every participant completed the task in both modalities.
 * Procedure:
   * Group A (N=21): Performed the Single Target Game AND Single Target Lab Task.
   * Group B (N=16): Performed the Multi-Target Game AND Multi-Target Lab Task.
   * Counterbalancing: Half played game first, half did lab tasks first.
3. Research Questions of Interest
Students should investigate the following:
 * RQ1 (Concurrent Validity): Is there a significant positive correlation between performance (RT/Accuracy) in the Game and the Lab Task?
 * RQ2: Is performance significantly worse in the Multiple Target condition compared to the Single Target condition?
 * RQ3: Does the gamified interface significantly alter performance compared to the standard lab interface?
 * RQ4: What is the effect of level in the games?
4. Statistical Metrics Required
Students should apply the following concepts:
 * Descriptive Statistics: Mean (M) and Standard Deviation (SD) for Reaction Time (RT) and Accuracy across all 4 cells.
 * Correlation: Pearson’s r (or Spearman’s \rho) to check the correlation between Game and Lab scores for each group (Validity check).
 * Hypothesis Testing:
   * Mixed ANOVA (2×2): To test the interaction between Modality (Within) and Target Load (Between).
   * Paired Samples t-test: To compare Game vs. Lab within each group.
   * Independent Samples t-test: To compare Single vs. Multi groups.
